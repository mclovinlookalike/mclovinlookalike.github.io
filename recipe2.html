Yellow Thai
Pork Curry

This recipe is meant to embody the many flavors and seasoning combinations that come from Thai culture. Me and my dad discovered some of this recipe on the internet during 2023, and over team we perfected the recipe to our liking and made it our own. Here is a somewhat similar recipe. This recipe seems to not be often founds in restaurant, and I think it would be better homemade anyways. Yellow curry is one of the least popular colored currys, but that doesnt mean it isn't good. Some recipes have more or less sauce, but my recipe uses the sauce as more of a glaze then a covering. Hopefully you enjoy it as much as me and my family do!

Description

This recipe has well cooked pieces of pork covered in delicious thai sauce and coconut milk. Inside, there are water chestnuts and baby corn to add texture, and peppers to add flavors. You can make it spicy with the right sauce, but at a base level the recipe is mild. The curry is best served hot, and can easily be reheated for lunch leftovers another day.

Ingredients

  • 2 LBS boneless porkchop
  • 1 package frozen pepper/onion blend
  • 1 8oz package sliced white mushrooms
  • 1 can of sliced water chestnuts
  • 2 cups of cooked jasmine rice
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 1 bottle of Trader Joe's yellow thai curry sauce
  • seasonings:
    • soy sauce
    • sriracha
    • black pepper
    • onion powder
    • garlic powder
    • ground ginger
    • paprika
    • rice vinegar
    • hoisin sauce

Directions

  1. Thinly slice the pork - sautee over med/high heat
  2. Add dry seasoning, soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and rice vinegar until cooked through
  3. add pepper and onion mix, mushrooms, and water chestnuts
  4. mix together in large frying pan or wok in heated sesame seed oil
  5. once all heated add the Curry sauce and half can of the coconut milk
  6. serve over jot cooked jasmine rice and finish with roasted sesame seeds
